## Key Observations

1. During gaps, network traffic does NOT stop. There are continuous low-volume
   exchanges (38-65 packets per 10s window) with NVIDIA GCP and AWS endpoints.

2. The traffic pattern during gaps shows ~30-second polling/keep-alive cycles
   with NVIDIA (34.36.57.103) and AWS (16.146.192.132). This suggests the
   NVIDIA inference proxy is making repeated calls or long-polling AWS Bedrock.

3. Anthropic us-east-1 endpoints (3.233.158.*) appear sporadically during gaps,
   suggesting the Claude CLI does make occasional auth/telemetry calls to
   Anthropic's infrastructure. However, these are infrequent (26 packets at
   most in one window) and don't correlate with gap start/end.

4. Gap resolution correlates with a large burst of traffic from NVIDIA/AWS,
   consistent with a delayed inference response finally arriving.

5. The 3.233.158.* IPs are in us-east-1 (Anthropic's infrastructure). The CLI
   contacts them during startup and sporadically during execution, but these
   connections are small and quick. They don't explain the 3-minute delays.

## Conclusion

The network data shows the Claude CLI IS waiting for a response during the gaps,
but the delay is on the NVIDIA/AWS Bedrock side, not from Anthropic auth calls.
The consistent low-volume traffic during gaps (30-65 packets per 10s with
NVIDIA and AWS) indicates an active long-lived HTTPS connection that is waiting
for a streaming response that takes ~3 minutes to arrive.

This contradicts the hypothesis that ANTHROPIC_AUTH_TOKEN triggers hidden auth
calls. The delay appears to be server-side queuing or rate-limiting at the
NVIDIA inference proxy or AWS Bedrock layer.
